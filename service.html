Main Papers Teaching & Service

I am a big believer that most humans can accomplish anything they care about (and that is known to be achievable by humans), given access to the right information and a supportive environment to learn and grow in. I hope my service and teaching in the past few years have been helpful with these!

Service

  • After COVID, I helped bring back the beloved Gems of TCS Reading Group and Theory Lunch at Princeton.
  • During my PhD, I served as the point of contact at Princeton for this crowdsourced spreadsheet that provides basic information about master’s programs in theoretical computer science — it was also how I first got started in research.
  • I have served as a reviewer for various conferences and journals, including EC, STOC, SODA, ITCS, WINE and Operations Research. I have yet to serve on a huge conference like NeurIPS or ICLR, it seems pretty dramatic!

Teaching

I have TA'd for the following courses at Princeton University. For more advanced courses, I generally like to give students a space on the board to collaborate, and they barely needed me!

  • COS 521 - Advanced Algorithms Design, Fall 2021.
  • COS 445 - Economics and Computing, Spring 2020.
  • COS 451 - Computational Geometry, Fall 2019.
  • COS 445 - Economics and Computing, Spring 2019.
  • COS 126 - Computer Science: An Interdisciplinary Approach, Fall 2018.

I have also served as a mentor for New Horizon TCS Summer School, a program aimed at exposing undergraduate students to research directions in theoretical computer science.
